## Context

Embedded control projects depend on clear technical direction across teams that may own systems, controls, software, diagnostics, calibration, safety, suppliers, compliance/risk topics, and delivery milestones.

## Technical challenge

The challenge was to align technical decisions, interfaces, requirements, constraints, risk topics, and delivery expectations across multiple stakeholders while keeping the intended control system behavior coherent.

## My role

My role was to drive technical alignment, clarify system expectations, structure discussions around tradeoffs, connect software and controls perspectives, and move implementation topics from intent toward robust execution.

## System scope

The scope covered requirements, interface expectations, control behavior, diagnostic behavior, software integration, supplier interaction, verification discussions, compliance/risk considerations, and delivery constraints.

## Standards / environment

The work took place in automotive embedded development contexts influenced by requirements processes, A-SPICE expectations, ISO 26262-oriented collaboration, AUTOSAR-oriented development, and production delivery discipline.

## Outcome

The result was stronger technical alignment around embedded control delivery, helping teams make progress across architecture, implementation, verification, supplier communication, stakeholder decisions, and production readiness.

## What this demonstrates

This demonstrates the ability to align teams, requirements, suppliers, standards expectations, and delivery constraints while keeping the embedded control system intent clear.
